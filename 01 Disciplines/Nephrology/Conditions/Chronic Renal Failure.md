The consequences of **renal failure** may be remembered using the mnemonic "MAD HUNGER":
- Metabolic Acidosis  
- Dyslipidemia (especially increased triglycerides)
- Hyperkalemia
- Uremia
- Na+/H2O retention
- Growth retardation and developmental delay
- Erythropoietin failure (anemia)
- Renal osteodystrophy


- Definition:
	- Generally defined as abnormality in kidney structure or function generally resulting in eGFR of ≤60
		- Having a kidney transplant means abnromality in kidney structure automatically classifying at CKD
- Classification
	- Cause, G1-G5 based on eGFR, Albuminaemia (A1-3) (CGA) classification has now surpassed the previous staging

# Management
- First line is lifestyle management
	- Screen for sleep apnoea in overweight/obese patients
- 4+3 approach
	- RAS-inhibitors
	- SGTL2-i
	- BP < 120
	- Statin
